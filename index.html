Quick Tips For Pest Prevention

10% OFF
First Service

Store Food Properly

Keep your food in airtight containers. This includes pet food, which sometimes people don't realize can attract unwanted guests.

Maintain Cleanliness

Your home needs clean, especially the kitchen. Wipe down surfaces, vacuum floors, and avoid leaving dirty dishes out overnight.

Declutter

Reduce clutter in the house and yard, as it makes the perfect hiding place for critters and rodents. Regularly get rid of things you don't need.

Trim Vegetation

Trim bushes, trees, and plants. This reduces the chances of wildlife making homes near your home.

Remove Debris

Clear away any debris, such as wood, leaves, or other natural materials, because these materials are used to make nests.

Use Natural Repellents

Consider using natural repellents like peppermint oil, vinegar, or diatomaceous earth. These can be applied in areas where you have seen activity.

Set Traps

Use traps for rodents, placing them in areas where you have seen activity. Be sure to check traps regularly and dispose of anything you catch quickly.

Regular Inspections

Do regular inspections of your property. A moist environment can create a dangerous breeding ground, so make sure places like your kitchen and washroom don't have any major leaks.

By following these DIY tips, you can significantly reduce the likelihood of serious infestations.

Common Methods of Pest Control

There are various methods for managing pests, each with its own advantages and disadvantages. Here's a breakdown of some common techniques:

Method Description Pros Cons
Chemical Control Involves the use of pesticides to eliminate. Quick results; effective for severe infestations. Potential health risks; environmental concerns; resistance development.
Biological Control Utilizes natural predators or parasites to control pest populations. Environmentally friendly; sustainable long-term solution. May take longer to see results; requires knowledge of ecosystem.
Mechanical Control Involves physical barriers or traps. Safe for humans and pets; no chemicals involved. Labor-intensive; may not be effective for large infestations.
Integrated Pest Management (IPM) A holistic approach combining multiple strategies for effective control. Reduces reliance on chemicals; promotes long-term prevention. Requires careful planning and monitoring; may be more complex.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much should I expect to pay for removal services?

Pest control costs vary depending on the type of pest and severity of infestation. Our pricing ranges from $150-$300 for ants, $200-$500 for rodents, $500-$1,500 for bed bugs, and $1,000-$2,500 for termites. We provide free quotes for accurate pricing specific to your situation.

What are the different types of services available?

We offer comprehensive pest control services including chemical control for quick elimination, biological control using natural predators, mechanical control with traps and barriers, and Integrated Pest Management (IPM) for long-term sustainable solutions.

What is the difference between pest control and exterminator?

Pest control refers to the broader management and prevention of pests through various methods, while an exterminator specifically focuses on eliminating existing pest infestations. Our services encompass both approaches for comprehensive pest management.

What is the best time of year to get rid of rats?

Fall and early winter are typically the best times for rodent control, as rats seek shelter indoors during colder months. However, we provide year-round services and 24/7 emergency response for immediate infestations regardless of season.

What are some tips for preventing mice in my home?

Key prevention tips include: storing food in airtight containers, maintaining cleanliness especially in kitchens, sealing entry points, removing clutter, trimming vegetation away from your home, and conducting regular property inspections for signs of activity.
